From the *About* page in the project: 

[**Visualizing History: The Clyde Malone Community Center**](http://malonecenterhistory.com/) was a research collaboration between the Clyde Malone Community Center and University of Nebraska-Lincoln's Digital Humanities Practicum class, carried out during the Spring semester of 2015.

We started the project with the recognition that the story of the social history of the Malone Community Center in Lincoln, NE had been for the most part invisible to members outside the community. We created the online visual exploration of the center’s changing locations, leadership, programs and activism hoping that it will deepen the understanding of the impact the various activities and projects of the center had on the black community in Lincoln from 1932 on, when it was first founded as a local chapter of the National Urban League by Trago McWilliams. 

[**Visualizing History** ](http://malonecenterhistory.com/) was also thought to encourage the voices of current community members to take part in curating, sharing, and analyzing their history. Sharing this content with users will enrich the appreciation of the community and inspire many more to become active members of the center, as well as contributing to the growing scholarship on social history in Lincoln, which seeks to approach the relationship between different social groups from the standpoint of community response and impact.

We also contributed to the digital humanities by using digital tools to narrate and visualize the history of the Malone Community Center that can serve as an example for future scholars .
